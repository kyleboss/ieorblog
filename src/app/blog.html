In Five Years...

In five years, I see myself as someone who has worked with Tech to improve the quality of life for humanity as a whole: rich or poor, black or white, queer or straight. I see myself continuing the fight to provide the tools necessary to navigate around destroy The Great Firewall of China so that the Chinese citizens are empowered with uncensored information. I don't wish to be known, famous, or rich. I wish to live simply, knowing that I have used my skill-set to create a tool that has empowered humanity. I see myself accomplishing this goal probably with people who have similar beliefs yet different skills. It probably does not involve me working for a Fortune 500 company or with a large disposable income; however, in five years, I will hopefully be able to take solace in knowing that I helped make a difference.

Strengths & Deltas

Strengths

Good with technology: I have an active passion for technology and its social implications. Currently a computer science major & a full-stack developer for a company in the bay. I aspire to use these skills to help build tools that empower all of humanity.

Leads positively: Prefer to give compliments to those who do a good job than to criticize. One catches more bees with honey than vinegar! This is how creativity thrives.

Doesn't procrastinate: Prefer to start early & get the ball rolling. Able to detect barriers early so there is no need to stay up late as deadlines approach. Often yields more quality work.

Enjoy public speaking: By no means am I a professional, but public speaking is an activity that I thoroughly enjoy and love improving. Great for all aspects in life such as conversations, projects, hackathons, etc.

Detail oriented: Modularize tasks to their basic components. I enjoy crossing things off of lists. This allows me to have a great sense of what it means to be "complete" when programming.

Deltas

Communication: I sometimes find it difficult to communicate technical ideas to technical folk. Often times it is because I taught myself a lot of the technical skills I know so I am not completely equipped with the technical vocabulary yet.

Trouble giving orders: I have a tough time giving orders when I lead. I fear of judgement and so sometimes my attempts at giving orders come off as passive aggression or just blunt. This gets me into trouble whenever I would like something done urgently.

Taking criticism: I can take criticism too personally. I sometimes question motives & sources of authority when a flaw is pointed out. Only recently have I been attempting to see criticisms as ways to improve & not flaws.

Bite off more than I can chew: I tend to take on more tasks than I can accomplish before a given deadline. I feel it is because during planning meetings I desire to be the "hero." As I begin to accomplish said tasks I quickly realize I under-estimated the time it would take.

Trouble seeing the "big picture": At times I modularize tasks so much that I have difficulty seeing the big picture of projects. I notice this especially when working on teams & it comes time to combine all of my tasks with the rest of the members' tasks.
